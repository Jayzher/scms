COIL Program

Collaborative Online International Learning: Connecting students globally through virtual exchange and cross-cultural collaboration.

About COIL Program

The Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL) Program at Holy Child Central Colleges is an innovative educational approach that connects students and faculty from different countries through technology-enabled learning. COIL integrates global perspectives into the curriculum, allowing students to engage in meaningful cross-cultural collaboration without the need for physical travel.

Our Approach

COIL courses are developed collaboratively by faculty from HCCC and partner institutions abroad. Students work together on shared projects, discussions, and assignments using various digital tools and platforms, creating a virtual mobility experience that enhances intercultural competence and global awareness.

Benefits

  • Develop intercultural communication skills
  • Gain global perspectives on academic subjects
  • Build international networks
  • Enhance digital literacy
  • Prepare for global workforce participation

How COIL Works

Course Development

Faculty from HCCC and partner institutions collaboratively design course modules with shared learning objectives and activities.

1

Key Elements

  • Aligned curriculum goals
  • Collaborative teaching approaches
  • Technology integration planning
  • Assessment strategies

Activities Include

  • Virtual introductions
  • Cultural exchange activities
  • Team formation
  • Project briefing
2

Student Orientation

Students are introduced to the COIL methodology, their international peers, and the digital platforms they will use for collaboration.

Collaborative Learning

Students engage in joint activities, discussions, and projects with their international counterparts using various digital tools.

3

Collaboration Tools

  • Video conferencing
  • Collaborative documents
  • Discussion forums
  • Project management platforms

Outcomes

  • Project presentations
  • Reflection papers
  • Cultural competence assessment
  • Digital portfolio development
4

Assessment & Reflection

Students complete assessments and reflect on their learning experiences, focusing on both academic content and intercultural competence development.
